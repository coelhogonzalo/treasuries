Tether International Limited, the company that launched and manages the stablecoin Tether (USDT), announced in "Tether’s Q1 2023 Assurance Report" that it would hold bitcoin as a reserve currency.(3). In August 2023, Bitcoin Magazine reported that Tether purchased an additional 1,529 bitcoin for 45.4M USD during the second quarter of the year.

Tether will regularly allocate up to 15% of its net realized operating profits towards purchasing Bitcoin (BTC)(1).

What Does Tether International Limited Do?

Tether International Limited is a provider of the stablecoin Tether.

This is a blockchain digital currency that is backed 1 for 1 with the fiat currency used for the purchase of the token.

Tether can be purchased in US dollars (USD), euro (EUR), Mexican Peso (MXN), and offshore Chinese yuan (CNH).

Represented by ₮, Tether tokens are denoted as USD₮, EUR₮, MXN₮, CNH₮. So $1 of Tether (USDT) will always be worth $1. That is why it is considered a stablecoin.

Who Owns Tether International Limited?

Tether International Limited is owned by iFinex Inc. a Hong Kong-based company that also owns the cryptocurrency exchange Bitfinex.

Why is Tether International Buying Bitcoin?

Tether International Limited is purchasing bitcoin as part of its company reserves.

What other Assets Does Tether Buy?

In addition to bitcoin purchases, Tether has invested a large percentage of assets in US Treasury Bills and gold. The remainder of the asset's value is held in cash, cash equivalents, and other short-term deposits. Tether is the largest private purchaser of U.S. Government Treasury Bills - only other governments buy more.(2)
